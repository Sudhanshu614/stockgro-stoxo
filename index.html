Why This Flow, and Not the Whole Product

Stoxo's value lives or dies in one single interaction:

A user types a question ‚Üí gets an answer they trust enough to act on.

Every other metric (retention, conversion to Stoxo PRO, NPS, revenue) is downstream of this atomic loop. If it works, everything else follows. If the answer is slow, vague, or feels like ChatGPT, they'll never come back.

Competitive Landscape - The Real Picture

The threat isn't Screener.in or ChatGPT. It's a two-front war.

Front 1: Brokers are embedding FREE* AI research inside trading platforms.
Front 2: Standalone AI research tools are building their own proprietary models.

Broker AI Threat (Bundled + Free + Trade-Enabled)

Broker AI What They Offer Why It Threatens Stoxo
Groww AI ~12.5M Active Users. In-house agentic AI model. Analyzes complete investment history, risk profiles. Can research, analyze, AND execute trades via natural language. Research ‚Üí Conviction ‚Üí Trade ‚Üí Track, all without leaving the app. It turns research into a "feature," not a "product."
Zerodha Kite MCP ~8M Active Users. Connects Zerodha to Claude/GPT via Model Context Protocol. Portfolio-personalized analysis, backtesting, and custom indicators. It lets users "Bring Their Own AI" (BYO-AI). Users get institutional-grade analysis for free by linking their own keys. Zero switching cost for Zerodha loyalists.

Why this is a two-front war:

  • ‚Ä¢ Broker AIs threaten with distribution (free, bundled, 27M+ active user base) + trade execution. Their advantage is structural - they own the transaction layer.
  • ‚Ä¢ Standalone AIs (Fuzz, LiMo, Multibagg, Tradl) threaten with specialized approaches - proprietary models, filing-level depth, quant screening. Their advantage is focus.
  • ‚Ä¢ Stoxo sits between these two forces: it has neither trade execution NOR a proprietary Indian-market AI model. But what Stoxo has that none of them do: 35M users' behavioral data from StockGro. (That's a proprietary dataset)

Where the Flow Breaks - Critical Gaps

These are not just feature requests; they are structural weaknesses in the query-to-action loop that cost user trust and revenue.

# Gap Where It Breaks What It Costs
1 No post-verdict actions After the analysis, the only option is "ask another question" Every session ends as a dead end; no save, no alert, no trade loop
2 Trust is invisible SEBI RA registration is a footer line nobody reads Users can't distinguish Stoxo from ChatGPT; kills willingness to pay
3 Wrong share unit "Share on WhatsApp" sends the entire conversation Chat logs don't go viral; verdict cards do
4 Cliff, not a funnel 5 lifetime free queries with no second chance One bad response taints the trial; user churns permanently
5 Portfolio-blind Stoxo doesn't know what the user already holds Broker AIs give personalized context for free
01

Gap 1: No Post-Verdict Actions

The moment: User reads a bullish TCS analysis with an 8/10 Stoxo Stock Score. They're convinced. Now what?

What's missing: Save to watchlist, set a price alert, share a verdict card, simulate in virtual portfolio, or execute a trade. None of these exist. The only CTA is "ask another question."

What it costs: Every session trains the user to treat Stoxo as disposable. No save equals no return trigger. No share equals no viral loop. No trade link equals zero revenue attribution.

Why broker deep-links aren't the answer (yet): Zerodha and Groww are building their own AI - they have no reason to partner. The pragmatic first move is in-ecosystem: "Add to Virtual Portfolio" / "Simulate This Trade" / "Copy Trade Details."

02

Gap 2: Invisible Trust Differentiation

The moment: User gets a TCS analysis and wonders, "Why should I trust this over ChatGPT?"

What's missing: The SEBI RA registration (INH000018300) is a footer line. No methodology followed. No backlinking to regulatory filings.

What it costs: Competitors like Fuzz cite regulatory filings as sources. If users can't feel the compliance difference, there's no reason to pay for Stoxo PRO over a competitor.

03

Gap 3: The Wrong Share Mechanic

The moment: User taps "Share on WhatsApp" and Stoxo sends the entire conversation (10+ messages of back-and-forth).

Why this is the wrong unit:

Whole Conversation Single Verdict Card
Recipient "Too long, didn't read" "Stoxo says 8/10? Why?" ‚Üí taps link
Branding Looks like a support log Visual, iconic, instantly recognizable
Social trigger "I chatted with a bot" "Look at this research I found"
CAC impact Near zero Every share = free branded acquisition

The fix: Keep "Share Conversation" for personal records. Make "Share Answer" the primary CTA - a branded Stoxo Stock Score snapshot that pulls recipients into the app. Stock tips already spread via unverified WhatsApp forwards in India. Stoxo's SEBI-registered card replaces those with a verified alternative.

04

Gap 4: The 5-Lifetime-Query Cliff

The moment: User finishes query #5. Screen goes blank. "Subscribe to continue."

What's broken: This is a cliff, not a funnel. Spotify lets free users listen forever (with ads). Notion gives unlimited pages. Stoxo gives 5 chances - ever. If even one of those 5 returned a weak response (wrong entity, generic answer, a stock they didn't care about), the entire trial is tainted.

What it costs: No second chance at a first impression. And with the user base who primary purpose is to learn things, users might exhaust all 5 queries without ever seeing the one moment that justifies paying.

05

Gap 5: The Portfolio Blind Spot

The moment: User asks "Should I buy ITC?" Stoxo returns a generic fundamental/technical analysis.

What's missing: Stoxo doesn't know what the user already holds. Broker AIs (Zerodha, Groww) give personalized context for free.

The opportunity: India's Account Aggregator frame enables consent-based data sharing. Stoxo could add risk context without crossing into SEBI IA territory as long as it frames this as risk information, not personalized advice.

Deep Dive: Top 2 Improvements

üü¢ Improvement 1: WhatsApp Verdict Card (P0)

The problem: Zero organic distribution. The current "Share" sends the entire conversation; nobody reads a chat log forwarded on WhatsApp.

The fix: "Share as Card" generates a branded image showing the verified technical and fundamental scores.

Key design choice: The card shows the score but NOT the Buy/Sell verdict. This is critical for two reasons:

  • SEBI compliant: No "advice" travels outside the platform.
  • Creates curiosity: "7.2/10... but is it a Buy or Hold?" ‚Üí user taps link.
  • Every share = free branded ad with a built-in conversion trigger.

Track: Shares per user per week (target: 0.5+), share-to-signup conversion, and K-factor.

üîî Improvement 2: Smart Alerts - Pull to Push (P1)

The problem: Stoxo is pull-only. Users must remember to open the app. There's no reason to return unless they actively think about it.

The fix: After any analysis, offer alert CTAs that pull the user back in based on real-time triggers:

  • "Alert me when TCS hits Rs.3,800" (price trigger)
  • "Notify me if Stoxo Stock Score drops below 7" (score trigger)
  • "Update me after Q4 results" (event trigger)

Why this is the retention unlock: A user with pending alerts will open Stoxo when notified; that's a guaranteed return visit driven by the product. This is how Stoxo becomes a habit instead of a one-time tool.

Track: Alerts set per user, alert-triggered return visits, and D7 return rate.
